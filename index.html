🎯

Address ALL parts of the question

Band 8 Task Response requires fully addressing every element. "Discuss both views AND give your opinion" means three distinct tasks. Missing any one caps you at Band 5 regardless of writing quality.

📐

Complex sentence variety

Mix conditionals, relative clauses, nominalisations, and passive constructions. Never start multiple sentences the same way.

❌ "This is important. This helps society."
✅ "Such developments, whilst beneficial in isolation, can fundamentally reshape..."
📖

Paraphrase vocabulary constantly

Each content word should appear at most twice in your entire essay. Build synonym networks before the exam.

important → crucial, significant, vital, paramount, fundamental
increase → surge, escalate, soar, rise sharply, climb
🔗

Vary cohesive devices

Avoid repeating the same linkers. Examiners notice "firstly, secondly, thirdly" used mechanically.

Addition: Furthermore · Moreover · In addition
Contrast: Nevertheless · Conversely · Notwithstanding
Result: Consequently · As a result · This, in turn,
📏

Word count discipline

Task 1: 160-190 words. Task 2: 270-290 words. Under the minimum is penalised. Over 350 wastes time and risks errors. Count before submitting.

🗺️

Task 1: Always write an Overview

The overview (2-3 sentences on the most striking overall trend) is mandatory for Band 6+. Place it as your second paragraph. Never describe every data point — identify the pattern.

✅ "Overall, internet usage rose consistently across all four countries, with Country A showing the most dramatic increase."
📋 Task 2 Essay Structures ▾

Opinion Essay: Intro (paraphrase + thesis) → Body 1 (main argument) → Body 2 (counterargument + refutation) → Conclusion

Discussion Essay: Intro → Body 1 (View A + evidence) → Body 2 (View B + evidence) → Conclusion (balanced + your view)

Problem/Solution: Intro → Body 1 (causes/problems) → Body 2 (solutions) → Conclusion

Advantages/Disadvantages: Intro → Body 1 (advantages) → Body 2 (disadvantages) → Conclusion (overall judgment)

📊 Task 1 Language Bank ▾

Upward trends: rose sharply, increased significantly, surged, climbed, escalated

Downward trends: fell dramatically, plummeted, declined, decreased substantially, dropped

Stable/Flat: remained relatively stable, levelled off, plateaued, showed little change

Comparisons: By contrast, In comparison, While X increased, Y fell..., Conversely

Approximation: approximately, roughly, just over/under, around, nearly

🗣️

Never give one-sentence answers

In Part 1, give 3-4 sentences. In Part 3, develop your idea, give evidence, then speculate. Examiners need to hear you sustain language use.

Q: Do you like cooking?
❌ "Yes, I do."
✅ "Absolutely — I find cooking genuinely therapeutic. There's something deeply satisfying about creating a meal from scratch, especially when I experiment with dishes from other cultures. Lately I've been trying to master Japanese cuisine, which is far more technical than I expected."
⏸️

Use natural fillers, not silence

Silent pauses hurt fluency. Natural thinking fillers sound fluent and buy thinking time.

Good fillers:
"That's an interesting question — let me think..."
"To be honest, I'd have to say..."
"I've never really thought about it, but I suppose..."
"Well, it depends really..."
🎨

Use idiomatic language

Natural idioms and fixed phrases demonstrate lexical resource. Use them when they arise naturally — not forced.

Useful phrases:
"It goes without saying that..."
"I'm in two minds about..."
"On balance, I'd have to say..."
"All things considered..."
"The pros clearly outweigh the cons..."
🔮

Speculate in Part 3

Part 3 rewards abstract thinking. Use hypothetical and speculative language.

Speculation phrases:
"It's conceivable that..."
"I would imagine that..."
"There's a strong argument to be made..."
"If I had to speculate, I'd say..."
"One could argue that..."
🔄

Self-correct naturally

Occasional self-correction is actually a Band 8 feature — it shows awareness and control. Don't panic over small slips. Just correct and continue confidently.

🏗️

Structure Part 2 clearly

You have 1 minute to prepare. Use the bullet points as a guide, but develop each into 2-3 sentences. End with how you feel about it. Speak for the full 2 minutes.

IELTS Writing Task 2 — Paragraph-by-Paragraph Guide
📝 Introduction (2-3 sentences) ▾

Sentence 1: Paraphrase the question topic (never copy words directly)
Sentence 2: State the essay type and your position (for opinion essays) or what you will cover

Band 8 intro example (Opinion Essay):
"The question of whether the internet has had a predominantly positive or negative impact on society continues to divide opinion. While some argue its benefits far outweigh its drawbacks, I firmly believe that the advantages of widespread connectivity are substantially greater than the associated risks."

📝 Body Paragraph 1 (5-7 sentences) ▾

Formula: Topic sentence → Explanation → Example/Evidence → Impact → Link back

Topic sentence: Introduce one clear main point
Explanation: Develop the idea — why/how
Example: Specific, concrete example (can be hypothetical)
Impact: What is the consequence/significance?
Link: Brief sentence connecting back to your thesis

📝 Body Paragraph 2 (5-7 sentences) ▾

Mirror the structure of Body 1 but with a different main point. For Discussion Essays, this paragraph should present the opposing view. Begin with a strong contrast linker:

"Conversely, those who oppose this viewpoint contend that..."
"On the other hand, proponents of the alternative perspective argue..."

📝 Conclusion (2-3 sentences) ▾

Never introduce new ideas in the conclusion.

Sentence 1: Restate your position (paraphrased, not copied)
Sentence 2: Summarise the key reasons
Sentence 3 (optional): Forward-looking statement or recommendation

"In conclusion, whilst the opposing view has some merit, I maintain that [your position]. The [reason 1] and [reason 2] outlined above suggest that [your stance]. Governments and individuals alike would benefit from [recommendation]."
